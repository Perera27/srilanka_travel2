🌊 Water & Beach Safety
  • Always swim at patrolled beaches with lifeguards present.
  • Respect red flag warnings — they indicate dangerous currents.
  • The south-west coast (Hikkaduwa, Unawatuna) can have strong rip currents during monsoon (May–September).
  • Never swim alone or at night.
🏛️ Cultural & Religious Sites
  • Remove shoes before entering temples and mosques.
  • Dress modestly — cover shoulders and knees. Sarongs are often available at temple entrances.
  • Never pose with your back to a Buddha statue for photos — it is considered disrespectful.
  • Ask before photographing religious ceremonies.
🐘 Wildlife Safety
  • Keep a safe distance from elephants — they can be unpredictable.
  • Do not feed wild animals in national parks.
  • Always stay inside your safari vehicle unless instructed otherwise.
  • Wear insect repellent, especially near forests and water bodies.
🌦️ Weather & Monsoon
  • South-west monsoon: May–September (affects west/south coasts).
  • North-east monsoon: November–February (affects east/north coasts).
  • During heavy rains, avoid hiking in slippery terrain or near waterfalls.
  • Check SLMC (Sri Lanka Meteorological Department) for weather forecasts.
🚗 Getting Around Safely
  • Use licensed taxis or PickMe / Uber for reliable rides.
  • Roads can be narrow and winding — allow extra travel time to hill country.
  • Always wear seatbelts and helmets when hired.
  • Keep a copy of your passport and travel insurance details.
